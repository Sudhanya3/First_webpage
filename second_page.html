Origin of SamaVeda

The Samaveda is believed to have been compiled around the same time as the Rigveda, between 1500 BCE and 1200 BCE. It is derived from the Rigveda and is closely related to it, with many of its verses borrowed directly from the Rigvedic hymns. While the Rigveda is primarily a collection of hymns in verse form, the Samaveda focuses on the musical aspect of these hymns, providing melodies and chants for use in rituals and ceremonies.
