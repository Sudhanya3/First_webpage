Origin of AtharvaVeda

The Atharvaveda is believed to have been composed around the same time as the other Vedas, between 1500 BCE and 1200 BCE. It is the fourth Veda and is considered to be a collection of knowledge associated with everyday life, including rituals, spells, prayers, and magical incantations. The Atharvaveda is named after the sage Atharvan, who is traditionally regarded as one of its composers.
